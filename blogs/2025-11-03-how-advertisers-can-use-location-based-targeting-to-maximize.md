<p>This year has been marked by economic uncertainty and changing consumer habits. From fluctuating confidence to unpredictable spending, many brands have had to stay agile. But amid all that change, one thing remains constant: <strong>location</strong>.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-why-location-brings-clarity-in-uncertain-times" style="font-size: 28px;">Why Location Brings Clarity in Uncertain Times</h2>



<p>Location-based targeting segments give advertisers the ability to reach consumers based on <strong>real-world behaviors and preferences</strong>, not just online clicks. These audiences reflect how consumers actually move, shop, and engage in the physical world, offering a more accurate picture of intent—even during times of shifting consumer behavior.</p>



<p>With location-based targeting, marketers can connect with consumers who are:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Actively visiting outlet malls, big-box stores, or other foot traffic hot spots</strong> in the final weeks of the winter holiday stretch</li>



<li><strong>Showing intent for budget-friendly options</strong>, shown through their recent visits to discount stores and value-focused retailers</li>



<li><strong>Signaling interest in your competitive set</strong> based on their real-time location or past visit history</li>
</ul>



<p>By segmenting audiences this way, advertisers can precisely target consumers most likely to act and avoid wasted impressions, influencing purchase decisions at critical moments and ensuring campaigns stay aligned with real-world activity. Plus, segments can be easily added to campaigns in-flight, allowing marketers to see immediate value without the need to rebuild or restart campaigns.</p>



<div class="wp-block-spacer" style="height: 35px;"></div>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-holiday-momentum-what-we-saw-last-year" style="font-size: 28px;">Holiday Momentum: What We Saw Last Year</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img alt="" class="wp-image-55151" height="565" src="https://foursquare.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2025/10/Q4-Blog_HolidaySales.gif" width="800" /></figure>



<p>Foursquare’s 2024 foot traffic data tells a clear story about where consumers were directing their attention and spending throughout the holiday season:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Toy Stores</strong> led growth from October to November <strong>(+26%),</strong> followed by <strong>Fashion Retail (+23%)</strong>, <strong>Cosmetic Stores (+21%)</strong>, and <strong>Gift Stores (+19%)</strong>.<br /></li>



<li>From November to December, momentum continued with <strong>Toy Stores</strong> leading the pack<strong> (+43%)</strong>, and <strong>Department Stores (+39%)</strong>, <strong>Cosmetic Stores (+37%)</strong>, and <strong>Fashion Retail and Sporting Goods (+36%)</strong> following close behind. <strong>Computer and Electronics Stores (+15%)</strong> and<strong> Big Box Stores (+20%)</strong> also saw a lift in foot traffic, but relatively smaller gains than other retail categories.<br /></li>



<li>During the week of <strong>Black Friday</strong>(compared to the prior week), outlet mall visits were <strong>61% higher</strong> than shopping malls. By the two weeks leading up to Christmas, that gap narrowed to just <strong>2.5%</strong>, showing less shopper preference later in the season.</li>
</ul>



<p>These shifts highlight how quickly consumer priorities evolve during the final stretch of the year. While last year’s trends provide a valuable baseline, staying connected to <strong>real-time behavioral data</strong> through privacy-forward location-based targeting segments ensures that your campaigns reflect how shoppers are actually acting <strong><em>today</em></strong>, not just how they behaved last season.</p>
