<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-what-fragmented-measurement-is-costing-you-and-how-to-fix-it" style="font-size: 26px;">What fragmented measurement is costing you and how to fix it</h2>



<p>Today&#8217;s consumer journey is anything but linear.</p>



<p>Consumers move seamlessly between channels, devices, and moments. They might discover a brand on social media, search for it later, see an ad on TV, and finally make a purchase in-store. This complexity has pushed marketers to adopt omnichannel strategies.</p>



<p>But here is the problem: with fragmented measurement systems, each channel along that journey can claim full credit for the same sale. The result is a measurement environment where the numbers look great on paper but bear little relationship to what actually happened.</p>



<p>This is the deduplication gap, and it is costing advertisers more than they realize.</p>



<div class="wp-block-spacer" style="height: 35px;"></div>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-why-fragmented-measurement-falls-short" style="font-size: 26px;">Why Fragmented Measurement Falls Short</h2>



<p>The complexity of modern consumer behavior has outpaced most measurement solutions. With 73% of shoppers using multiple channels before making a purchase, and consumers interacting with 6-8 touchpoints before converting, the path to purchase runs across social, search, TV, OOH, audio, and more.</p>



<p>Yet most measurement solutions still analyze performance channel by channel. When campaigns reach the same audience across platforms, fragmented reporting allows each platform to claim credit for the same visit or purchase. The outcome is duplicated attribution, inflated ROI, and media investment decisions built on inaccurate data.</p>



<p>Without proper deduplication, advertisers risk:</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-what-deduplicated-measurement-actually-means" style="font-size: 26px;">What Deduplicated Measurement Actually Means</h2>



<p>Deduplication comes down to one principle: every conversion gets counted once.</p>



<p>When measurement is deduplicated, each real-world outcome, whether a store visit or a purchase, is counted once, even when multiple touchpoints influenced the conversion. Credit is distributed across the full path proportionally, within a single unified framework, rather than claimed in full by each platform independently.</p>



<p>When conversions are counted accurately, marketers can identify the true drivers of visits and sales, allocate spend with confidence, and make optimization decisions based on what is actually working rather than what appears to be working.</p>

Rather than allowing multiple channels to claim the same conversion in full, credit is shared proportionally, eliminating double-counting across channels, partners, tactics, and conversion types.</p>

<p>In a media environment where every dollar must prove its value, measurement accuracy is not a nice-to-have. It is the foundation of every investment decision you make.</p>
